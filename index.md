## 👩‍🔬 About Me

I am a **GIS & Remote Sensing specialist** and **Geo-AI researcher** based in Lahore, Pakistan, with an MS from NUST IGIS and over 7 years of experience in higher education administration. My work sits at the intersection of **spatial data science**, **machine learning**, and **environmental monitoring** — translating complex geospatial datasets into actionable insights.

My MS thesis investigated the **spatial distribution of bio-aerosols** at NUST's H-12 campus, analysing how land use/land cover patterns and atmospheric conditions influence pollen and fungal spore densities. I am now re-framing this original fieldwork data as a **GeoAI reanalysis** — applying Random Forest, XGBoost, and spatial ML techniques to generate reproducible, peer-review-ready outputs.

> 🎯 Currently building this portfolio to support **funded PhD applications in Europe** in Geo-AI, environmental informatics, and spatial intelligence.

## 🔬 Featured Research Project

### ⭐ GeoAI Bioaerosol Prediction — NUST Campus
> *Real fieldwork data · Spatial ML · Original research*

[![Repo](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-geoai--bioaerosol--prediction-2D6A4F?style=flat-square&logo=github)](https://github.com/Filza-coder/geoai-bioaerosol-prediction)
[![Notebook](https://img.shields.io/badge/Colab-EDA%20Notebook-F9AB00?style=flat-square&logo=googlecolab&logoColor=white)](https://github.com/Filza-coder/geoai-bioaerosol-prediction/blob/main/notebooks/01_EDA_BioAerosol_NUST.ipynb)

**Research question:** How do land use/land cover variables and micrometeorological conditions predict atmospheric bioaerosol concentrations across urban campus environments?

**What I did:**
- Collected original field data at **6 sampling sites** across NUST H-12 campus
- Measured daily pollen and fungal spore concentrations across **12 sampling days**
- Applied **Random Forest** to identify LULC predictors of pollen and fungus density
- Performed **IDW spatial interpolation** to generate campus-wide concentration maps
- Confirmed meteorological correlations (e.g., wind speed r=0.60 at Site 2)

**Key ML findings:**
| Target | Top Predictor | Insight |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Pollen concentration | Building Density | Built surfaces suppress airflow |
| Fungus concentration | Grass Land % | Vegetation drives spore release |
| Spatial hotspot | Site 2 (highest pollen) | Open corridor effect confirmed |

**Stack:** Python · Pandas · Scikit-learn · Matplotlib · Geopandas · Scipy · Jupyter
